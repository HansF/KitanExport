However, the other day, passing the front of this shop, a poster was stuck all over the large display window, and there was a mountain-like crowd of people. Wondering what it was, I pushed through the wave of people and approached...

"American-style Easy Payment Sales! For only 500 yen a month, a 12,000 yen R sewing machine can be obtained. Now in a big sale with a grand prize of 300,000 yen."

...it was written.
If translating Easy Payment, it is "easy payment method," in other words, installment sales. If it is about 500 yen a month, even for my poor household, I can somehow manage.

I immediately entered the shop in high spirits, caught a clerk who was receiving customers while dripping with sweat, and received one application form. Thinking that if I paid in 500 yen, from tomorrow, a shiny glittering sewing machine would be installed in my home, I returned home full of hope and immediately consulted my wife at the dinner table.
My wife, with a suspicious face, said:
"But, 12,000 yen for a sewing machine in cash vs. 500 yen every month for 24 payments is strange. If it's installments, because of interest and such, the installments should be higher, or else, the number of payments should be more."
So she said. I also realized with a start:
"That's true. If there's no interest and on top of that they give a 300,000 yen prize... the cost price of this machine must be absurdly cheap and poor quality. Shall we read that application form well once more?"
And, when I read the regulations well, I was disappointed.

It was indeed a 500 yen payment for two years (24 months) until maturity, but it was written that the essential item would be handed over *after* paying everything in full.
"What, if that's the case, their scheme is to freely use other people's money for two years interest-free! They're making a fool of us!" I see, there's no easy profit like this."
"To not get caught in such a fake thing, as expected, if I save by myself every month and endure for about two years, with interest it will become about 15,000 yen. With that, let's buy a good sewing machine freely with cash. Surely the price will have become even cheaper by then."
