**Amusement District Bogus Boogie-Woogie**
**Kado Kotaro**

**Episode 1: A Wonderful Find**

Autumn nights are long, but when drinking, the passage of time is not known.
"Huh? It's already 11 o'clock. If I miss the last Hankyu train, it'll be terrible. Well, excuse me!"
I greeted my bad friends, hugged my bag, and hurriedly rushed out of the bar. Since it was the backstreets of Sonezaki, Umeda was right there. As expected for October, it was damply chilly. As usual, descending the stairs of the underground passage, as I was walking briskly through a dim area, suddenly from behind,
"Excuse me, hey,"
I was called out to. There were no other figures around, so it must be me. It wouldn't be a highwayman, surely; I stopped suddenly while being on guard.
"Is this your watch, sir?"
The one who approached, putting a round, flat object wrapped in a handkerchief on his hand, was a dirty man looking like a *lumpen* (homeless person). I also have a pocket watch, a memento from my late father. Being told that, reflexively I searched my vest pocket. Phew, a relief; my watch was ticking away safely.
"No, thanks, it wasn't mine."
"Is that so? Hmm, it's a good watch. Look, sir, it's definitely gold-cased. Don't know who dropped it, but it's a wonderful valuable item."
Drawn by the lumpen's admiration, I also peered in. Though under the dim underground passage light, it glinted like pure gold.
"Sir, about how much value does it have?"
"Well, if it's pure gold, it would be worth 10,000 yen."
"10,000 yen? Is that so? It's foolish to just hand it to the police and get a 500 or 1,000 yen reward..."
Quickly glancing around, the lumpen lowered his voice and whispered to me.
"Sir, how about it? Only you and I know. Let's sell this and split it 5,000 yen each. Hehehe."
Embezzling a found item isn't good. However, being drunk, I was suddenly tempted. When I nodded silently,
"Then, sir. If a lumpen like me goes to sell it, I'll surely be suspected. It's a bother, but sir, please go sell this at the watch shop just up from the underground passage. They should still be awake, and if it's a gentleman like you, they'll feel safe and turn it into cash right away..."
To the lumpen who said he would wait here, I entrusted my bag, took that watch, and hurried to the watch shop as instructed.
"I've lost my travel money and am in trouble; won't you buy this watch?"
When I said that, the watch shop old man gave a quick "yes" and said,
"Well then, let me take a good look,"
and began examining that watch under a bright desk lamp.
10,000 yen? 15,000 yen? My heart pounding, I waited for the old man's reply.
