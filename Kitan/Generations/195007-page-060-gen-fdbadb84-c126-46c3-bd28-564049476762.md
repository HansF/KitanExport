**[Chapter 2] The Samurai Who Pledged with a Corpse**

Long ago. There was a single poor samurai. For a long time, throughout his poverty, he had no way to serve a lord and lived in misfortune. However, because he heard that his friend had become the governor of a certain province, he went out immediately.
That helper met him kindly. And:

For him, for several years, there was a wife he had become familiar with through visiting. She was still young in years, was the possessor of considerable beauty, and since her heart was good and she was lovely, she had lived with him without even minding his poverty. But when it came to going down to a far province, he threw that woman away like a worn-out shoe.
Finally, when it was decided to set out on the journey, he made another woman convenient for traveling his wife and departed Kyoto early with that woman.
When he settled in the place of appointment, for him, it was an unexpected, comfortable life according to his heart.

Thus, as he spent peaceful months and days, that which came to be remembered in the depths of his heart was the matter of the original wife whom he had silently abandoned alone in Kyoto.
To begin with, she was not a woman he had come to hate and discarded. When he remembered, she was a nostalgic woman to the point of pain. The more he thought, the more the longing became nothing but intense.
"I want to see her. I want to see that woman."
Such nearly mad longing for the previous wife was only increased. That, gradually, into only pain, tormented his heart.
