## Literal English Translation

**The Mantis of the Axe**

To imitate something like a scene from an old-fashioned human-interest play feels, I must say, a bit too paltry. If I may be so bold as to offer an amateur's opinion, I wish there had been more twisted psychological descriptions, such as "adopting a 'pose' to annihilate one's own shame, while pointedly shielding others," or something of that sort. That would be more realistic, and one could glimpse the degree of shame; if it is the kind of "literature pursuing humanity" that some elite readers often speak of, then surely such things must be done. Well, Mr. Dan himself says things that are quite thrilling to my ears, like "I'm just scribbling these down with the intention of them being ero-novels," and no matter how much cheeky talk I spout, people like us could never write such things, and in the first place, we lack the creative power to bring it to this level. But Mr. Dan is a genius who has written many works besides this masterpiece. This is where a pro is a pro, but simply reading one work, it is not easy to distinguish whether it is the work of a professional or an amateur.

If I am told, "That’s because you don't read seriously, because you lack reading comprehension," I would be at a loss for an answer, but I believe that for this kind of material, it is better not to read too seriously to give a proper evaluation, and anyway, since I don't understand it, there's nothing to be done. Of course, distinguishing between pro and amateur, or "fake" and "real," is completely impossible. In the first place, does such a distinction even exist? Is it a "fake" if the writer is not a maniac (lunatic) for that particular path? Surely no one would say something so ridiculous. If that were the case, works where men handle female psychology would be fakes, works where general authors handle surgeons would be fakes, and all fiction would become fake—and more than anything, such a distinction is one that has nothing to do with the work itself.

In that case, do we call things we cannot understand or things that are not realistic "fakes"? If so, then "Camera Hunt" and "Camera Repo" (reportage) would also become complete fakes. There are likely many naive readers who believe these are all descriptions of reality, but I do not think so. I believe that efforts have been made to make them worth reading, and they likely contain a good deal of exaggeration. For example, in "Camera Hunt," there were scenes to drool over where someone "pissed all over a bitch (female slave) and indulged in the height of sadism" two or three times, but any experienced man knows that when a man is excited, no matter how hard he tries, only a tiny bit comes out. I don't know about impotent people, but at least for me, it is an impossible feat; generally speaking, it is not a realistic description, and physiologically, it is a great misconception. However, if one were to write such things with total honesty, it would become a laughingstock.

Non-fiction, in a sense, requires more hardship and technique than creative writing. It sounds strange to call it "the technique of lying," but in fact, such technique is greatly needed. Even if the woman doesn't look like much in the photo, you must write so she feels like a goddess in the text, or the readers won't be satisfied; and strangely enough, even the models, who are supposed to be "bitches" enjoying their masochism, start making egoistic complaints. The point is whether it is worth reading or not. Those without literary power cannot write things worth reading.
