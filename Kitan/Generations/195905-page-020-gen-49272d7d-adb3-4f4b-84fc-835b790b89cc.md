## Cultural & Historical Annotations

*   **Currency (1500 Yen):** In the mid-1950s, 1500 yen was a significant sum. For context, a starting salary for a college graduate might be around 10,000 to 15,000 yen per month. Spending 10-15% of a monthly wage on a single date explains Kenji's extreme resentment (復讐心 - *fukushū-shin*).
*   **Okachimenko (おかちめんこ):** A derogatory slang term for an unattractive woman, particularly one with a flat or "homely" face. It derives from "Okachi-machi" and "Menko" (flat cards). Using this word highlights the character's vanity and cruelty toward the artist.
*   **The Artist-Model Dynamic:** A recurring trope in *Kitan Club* is the "Pygmalion" or "Svengali" fantasy, where a male creator exerts control over a female subject through art, often involving physical restraint or "shaping" the woman to fit a specific aesthetic of suffering or beauty.

## Lexical Notes & Euphemisms

*   **ワ / ノ (Wa / No):** Written in katakana, these sentence-ending particles denote a "Modern Girl" (Moga) or "Arisuto" (Aristocrat) affectation, signaling Yukari's perceived superiority and flighty, demanding nature.
*   **手を入れる (Te o ireru):** Literally "to put hands in" (to touch/work on). In an artistic context, it means to retouch or finish. In the context of *Kitan Club*, it is a double entendre for physical manipulation or assault.
*   **畜生 (Chikushō):** "Beast" or "Damn it." A common, harsh masculine profanity.
*   **石膏像 (Sekkō-zō):** Plaster bust. In the ero-guro genre, stone or plaster often represents the "ideal," unmoving, and controllable version of a woman.
