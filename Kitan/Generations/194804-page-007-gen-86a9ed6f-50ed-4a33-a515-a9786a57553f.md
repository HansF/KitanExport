## Cultural Notes

*   **Tobita Shinchi (飛田遊廓):** One of Japan's most famous and largest red-light districts, located in Nishinari-ku, Osaka. It survived the 1958 Prostitution Prevention Law by rebranding brothels as "Japanese restaurants" (ryotei).
*   **Pan-pan (パンパン):** A slang term for street prostitutes in post-war Japan, often associated with those who catered to the Allied Occupation forces.
*   **Buseji (X):** The use of "X" or "○" to censor names was common in pulp magazines to avoid libel or police crackdowns while still providing enough detail for "those in the know."
*   **7-1 Stop (七一ストップ):** Likely refers to a specific municipal or police ordinance/crackdown period (July 1st) regarding cafe "special services" or unlicensed prostitution.
*   **Oshima Silk (大島紬):** A very expensive, high-quality traditional silk. The mention that the exhibitionist wears this suggests he is not a vagrant, but a man of means with a specific compulsion.
*   **Matsutake (松茸):** A common euphemism for the penis due to its shape.

## Lexical Notes

*   **Ryoki (猟奇):** Seeking the bizarre, grotesque, or macabre. A core keyword of the Era.
*   **Hentai (変態):** In this era, it meant "abnormal" or "perverted" in a more clinical/sociological sense than the modern anime-associated term.
