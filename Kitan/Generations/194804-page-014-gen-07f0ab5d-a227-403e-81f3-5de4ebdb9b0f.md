## 6ï¸âƒ£ Cultural Notes & Historical Context

*   **Kasutori Magazines:** Named after a cheap, foul-smelling shochu made from sake lees, "Kasutori" magazines were short-lived, low-quality publications that flooded the market in post-WWII Japan. They focused on "Three S" themes: Sex, Screen, and Sport, catering to a populace seeking escapism from the hardships of the occupation era.
*   **Pink Schoolgirls (Momo-iro Jogakusei):** The term "pink" in Japanese often denotes eroticism. Stories about "delinquent" or sexually active schoolgirls were a staple of the era, reflecting a societal anxiety/obsession with the changing morals of the younger generation under Western (American) influence.
*   **Post-War Commuting:** The description of being crushed in trains "like a cracker" reflects the extreme overcrowding of Japanese public transit in the rebuilding years, a trope that continues in Japanese media today.
*   **Medicine Salesmen (Kusuri-uri):** The "perverted thief" is identified as a traveling medicine salesman. Historically, these salesmen traveled from village to village, and because they entered private homes, they were often the subject of urban legends or suspicious gossip.
