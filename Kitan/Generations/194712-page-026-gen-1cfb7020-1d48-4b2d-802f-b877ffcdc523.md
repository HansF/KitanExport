*   **Structure:**
    *   **The Setup:** The "contract" and the trip to Takedao Onsen.

*   **Perspective:** First-person (僕), portraying himself as a passive victim of both the woman's desire and the family's wealth, which is a common narrative strategy to allow the reader to enjoy the decadence without feeling the protagonist is "evil."
*   **Target Audience:** Likely readers of *Kitan Club*—men who enjoyed stories of "perverse" family dynamics, submissive male roles, and the cynical intersection of money and sex.

## Cultural & Historical Annotations

*   **Tachibana Family (立花家):** A common name for "old money" or aristocratic families. The contrast between the wealthy *Yamanote* (uptown) and the *Shitamachi* (downtown) highlights class dynamics.
*   **Takedao (武田尾):** A real hot spring area near Osaka, historically known for being a somewhat secluded, "inner parlor" getaway.
*   **"Crippled Girl" (不具娘 - Fugu musume):** In the parlance of the era, this likely refers to a physical disability or a perceived mental/social fragility that kept her "sequestered." In *Ero-Guro* literature, physical "imperfection" was often linked to "abnormal" or "obsessive" sexual drive.
*   **Class A (甲種合格):** The highest fitness rating for the Imperial Japanese Army draft. This was effectively a death sentence toward the end of the war.
*   **New Guinea (ニューギニヤ):** One of the most brutal fronts of WWII for Japanese soldiers, characterized more by starvation and disease than actual combat. Mentioning survival from New Guinea marks the narrator as a man who has seen the absolute bottom of human existence.
*   **Bibliography (Bottom Right):** The smaller text discusses the publishing history of *Niku-buton* (The Meat Cushion), a famous erotic novel based on a Chinese classic.
