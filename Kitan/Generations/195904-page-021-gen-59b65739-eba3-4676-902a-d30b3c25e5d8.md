Ryubi: "Eh, what a wretched thing..."

Regarding Emperor Buretsu and [Toyotomi] Hidetsugu, such documents are not many, and it is only written in *Nihon Shoki* or *Taikoki* that they cut open the bellies of pregnant women.
In *Nihon Yoshi*, it says:
"Once going out, [he] saw a pregnant woman from afar and said: 'I hear that in ancient times there was one who cut open the womb. That pleasure can be imagined.' Therefore, he ordered his attendants, saying: 'Bring her here quickly.' One person ran and reached her, and whispered in the woman's ear: 'Just go away quietly.' Then [he] grasped the ears of wheat by the roadside, and returning to report, said: 'This person was carrying wheat straw in her bosom and was not pregnant. Therefore, I respectfully took a handful to prove this.' Thus, the woman was in danger but escaped."
So it is written, and it only says the woman was saved. In the *Yoshi*, almost the same article is written about Lord Tadanao. Therefore, it is unknown to what extent it can be trusted.
