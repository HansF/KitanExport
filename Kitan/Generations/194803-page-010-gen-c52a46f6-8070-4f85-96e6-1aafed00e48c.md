The mention of "military pensions being abolished" places this firmly in the Allied Occupation period (late 1940s), as the GHQ abolished military pensions in 1946 (they were later reinstated in 1953).
*   **The Linga (リンガ):** A loanword from Sanskrit (Shiva's phallus). In Showa-era pulp, "Linga" was used as a pseudo-medical or "exotic" euphemism for the penis to bypass certain obscenity sensibilities while remaining clear to the reader.
*   **Sekai (世界):** An actual intellectual magazine founded in 1946. Its mention suggests the narrator considers himself a student or an intellectual, adding a "refined" layer to the tawdry story.
*   **Koharu-biyori (小春日和):** "Indian Summer" or "Little Spring." It sets a mood of deceptive warmth before the "chilly" revelation.
