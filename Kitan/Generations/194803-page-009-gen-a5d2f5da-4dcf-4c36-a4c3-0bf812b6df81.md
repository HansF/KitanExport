## Cultural & Historical Notes

*   **Housing Shortage (住宅難 - Jutaku-nan):** In post-WWII Japan, major cities like Kobe were devastated by bombing. Housing was extremely scarce, and "room seeking" (room hunting) was a common struggle for veterans and young workers.
*   **Demobilization (復員 - Fukuin):** The protagonist is a *fukuin-sha*. This identity is central to the era's literature—men trying to reintegrate into a society that had changed, often feeling emasculated or out of place.
*   **Sumaura (須磨浦):** A scenic coastal area in Kobe. Choosing this setting suggests a middle-class or "high-collar" (haikara) environment, contrasting with the protagonist's poverty.
*   **Mitsu-yubi (三つ指):** A formal bow where the thumb, index, and middle fingers touch the floor. It signifies extreme politeness and traditional feminine grace.
*   **Venus Statue:** A common trope in Showa-era "modern" interiors, symbolizing Westernized taste and, in the context of *ero-guro* literature, a hint of latent eroticism or fetishism of the female form.

*   **精力型 (Seiryoku-gata):** "Energetic type" or "Virile type." In this context, it often hints at the husband's sexual potency, setting up a contrast with the protagonist or a potential threat/rivalry.
*   **艶然 (Enzen):** Bewitching, adoring, or radiantly beautiful. A highly charged aesthetic term.
