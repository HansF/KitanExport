## 6ï¸âƒ£ Cultural Notes & Historical Context

*   **Tsuruya Nanboku IV (1755â€“1829):** The most famous playwright of the late Edo period. He is the master of the *Kizewamono* genre (raw, realistic plays about the lower classes) and *Kaidan* (ghost stories). *   **Kizewamono (Raw Domestic Plays):** Unlike the stylized, heroic *Jidaimono* plays, these focused on the gritty, often violent lives of commoners, criminals, and outcasts (*hinin*).

*   **Nise-en (Mismatched Fate):** A recurring trope where a character commits a crime (murder/robbery) only to find out the victim was their long-lost child or parent. This maximizes the tragedy and "karmic" weight of the scene.
*   **Edobei/Fujikawa Mizuemon:** This refers to the villain of the play *DokudochÅ« GojÅ«santsugi*. Nanboku often reused character names and archetypes.
