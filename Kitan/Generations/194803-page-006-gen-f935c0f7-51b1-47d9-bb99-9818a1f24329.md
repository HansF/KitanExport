## Cultural & Historical Annotations

*   **Post-War Poverty:** References to "steamed potatoes instead of bread" and "30-yen kasutori" (a notorious, often toxic, low-grade liquor) highlight the desperate economic state of the late 1940s.
*   **Democratic Cynicism:** The author equates "Cheap and convenient" with "Democracy." This reflects a common post-war sentiment where the lofty ideals of the US occupation were often parodied or reduced to the freedom to pursue base desires.
*   **Pan-pan (パンパン):** Street prostitutes who catered to both GIs and Japanese men. The mention of "All night OK" and "no rotation" (no sharing) refers to the logistics of the black-market sex trade.
*   **Sano Jirozaemon:** A reference to the kabuki play *Kagotsurube Sato no Eigaza*, where the protagonist is a wealthy but hideously scarred man. The author suggests that even an ugly man can feel like a "Sultan" in the crowded train.

*   **カス取り (Kasutori):** Literally "dreg-pressing." A potent, low-quality shochu made from sake lees. Symbolic of the post-war "scorched earth" era.
*   **ホルモン (Hormone):** In this era, "hormone" was slang for sexual vitality, stamina, or libido (similar to how "testosterone" might be used today).
*   **ワクチャク (Wakuchaku):** An older slang term meaning "messy," "confused," or "ruined."
*   **一糸まとわぬ (Isshi matowanu):** "Without a single thread." Standard literary euphemism for total nudity.
*   **アトラクト (Atorakuto):** Gairaigo (loanword) for "Attract." Used to give a "modern" or "clinical" feel to the sensation of being smelled by schoolgirls.
