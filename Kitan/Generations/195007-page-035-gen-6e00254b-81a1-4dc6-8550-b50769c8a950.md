**Strange Story: The Ink Chastity Belt**

Meiji 11, May. Fukagawa Ward, Morishita-cho rickshaw puller Hagiwara Rinzo (30) was an extremely serious/honest man; every day before going to work, on wife Okatsu's (25) somewhere, with ink he made a mark, and when returning home, thoroughly inspected it, and then again made a mark and went out. Wife also, although it was foolish and she thought it annoying, this woman being a famously honest woman, let him do as husband does; however, recently, Rinzo's younger brother named Kamekich moved to the same town, and to his coming over to play, the older brother was greatly disapproving.

From then on, not finishing with just once at noon, returning three or four times a day, if she was by the hearth he would become very angry and vent his rage, "The smell is strange," "The color is suspicious," like a poor hygienist buying beef during the Midsummer Day of the Ox, noisily grumbling; Okatsu, unable to endure it, after four or five days fled to relatives in Honminato-cho, and explaining the details said "I absolutely cannot bear this," just at that time Rinzo returned for the usual inspection. Because Okatsu was not there, like a madman he searched various places, finally came to this house, as soon as he saw his wife's face he barged in shouting, and tried to take her hand and lead her home, but a certain person stopped him and explained his various misunderstandings, then Rinzo without a word apologized and returned. However, after that, the aforementioned inspection became even stricter, and after that he wouldn't let her go to work and from morning was mouth-noisy with insults, so as expected Okatsu's affection ran out, and the day before yesterday she appealed to the authorities. This wife's complaint is reasonable. (From Tokyo Nichi Nichi Shimbun)

When Funaoka Rokunosuke was released from Sakai Prison, it was the third day of the Island Festival. The street scenery seen for the first time in five years was entirely new, and Rokunosuke unintentionally widened his eyes. Island women's "perms" which one could say were rarely seen five years ago, recently popular long skirts, women wearing thin fabric dresses exposing skin. Those danced eerily in his vision. For a long time, having been in a world isolated from the opposite sex, in Rokunosuke's eyes every passing woman looked extraordinarily beautiful. His lust, which had its outlet tightly suppressed, was spurred by five years of restraint and he couldn't control it as it overflowed like a spring. The name of the crime for which he entered prison was attempted robbery-murder against a young housewife.

Rokunosuke, threading through the crowds of the Island Festival, hurried to his own house on the coast road. Even calling it a house, it was a corner of a crumbling tenement like a stable behind the shopping street, but there, Sayumi, whom he had separated from after just one month [of marriage], was supposed to be living alone. At an unfamiliar barber shop he shaved off his stubble and jumped into the tenement; what was the first thing reflected in Rokunosuke's eyes? It was a child's rattle hung with a wind chime. Instantly he wondered if it was someone else's house, but that couldn't be. With eyes full of suspicion rolling around, he stepped over the threshold. Inside, a woman in a single dirty chemise was lying down with an infant.

"Who?!" The woman looked back. "Ah!"
"Sayumi, whose child is that!" Rokunosuke, staring at the infant, jumped up onto the tatami floor with a creak. Sayumi's agape mouth suddenly twisted, and her haggard face became pale. "You, you've come back."
"Is it bad that I've come back?!" "No, but, wasn't your prison term eight years?"
Rokunosuke was looking around the room, and when he discovered a man's clothing and hat, he turned eyes of sharp hatred toward the infant. It was barely a year after birth. "You, you betrayed me."
"Betrayed... it's not like that, but, think about it... do you think a woman can live alone in these times? While you committed such a loathsome crime that ruined my reputation, do you think I'd wait eight years for that man to come out of prison?"
"What, hey, you've got a lot of nerve saying that." Rokunosuke aggressively approached her.
"Yes, I'll say it as much as I want. Originally, even this tenement, wasn't it my dead father who rented it? You lot, as if waiting for my father to die, barged in and made a mess of me, didn't you?"
"Humph, are those words to say to a husband returning after five years?"
"Who's calling someone like you [husband]... ridiculous. I have a proper husband named Kinji."
Sayumi said coldly and turned her face away. "Fool!"
Rokunosuke grabbed a kettle that was on a nearby shichirin (brazier) and threw it. The water was cold, but it splashed off Sayumi's body and drew a map on the dirty tatami. At that noise, the infant woke up and began crying like a house on fire. Sayumi, protecting the infant while backing away, screamed hysterically, "This is my house, get out!"
Rokunosuke's brutality robbed him of self-control, and he struck Sayumi regardless of where. Sayumi, dripping bright red nosebleed, wailed and cried. The neighbors who jumped out surprised by the commotion said, "Isn't that Rokunosuke?" "A bad guy has come back," "This will be interesting when Kin-san gets back," each saying so, but since other people's problems are entertaining, they didn't try to stop it at all.
"What's happened?" a man came jumping in. "Ah, it's Kin-san."
It was Kinji, who had unfortunately returned from his workplace for a meal. Seeing Sayumi being beaten, he ran in like a bolting rabbit and stood in Rokunosuke's way.
