**Now is past...** (Once upon a time...)

...therefore, [he] decided to go up to Kyoto together. His heart went to Kyoto, to Kyoto.
"What kind of man am I? That good-natured woman, for no reason, I abandoned [her] completely. When I arrive in Kyoto, I will immediately visit the wife I abandoned and make her my wife again."
He firmly vowed in his own heart. As soon as he arrived in Kyoto, on those feet (immediately), he visited the house of his former wife.
That place, compared to the time he saw it before, was completely different, entirely ruined and decayed. Grass was overgrown to the point there was no place for feet to step, a state of ruin so much that there was not even a hint of a person living there.
He saw this condition, more and more felt sorrow and self-reproach, and his heart pained.
Because it was around the 10th day of September, the moon was bright. Dew placed on grass leaves coldly wet the hem of his kimono. Opening the door and entering briskly to see, in the place where she always was, now just one person was sitting dejectedly.
Seeing him who had entered, she did not show even a bit of a resentful color, and spoke looking very happy.
"Oh, you, today did you return to this direction? I was lonely."
The man, during the time he was in a far country, spoke of the feelings he had held.
"The things I brought back from my post, within today or tomorrow I will have brought here. I will call servants too. Tonight I thought to just talk of this, so I came first thing."
She was happy at his words, and while they talked together of the sad life during the long time they were separated until now, the night deepened, so "Then, let's go to sleep," and the two holding hands moved to the south-facing room.
"In here, is there no one else?"
"Yes, because I could not even have a human-like life, there is not even one servant."
The two talked through the night until dawn, and around the time when the night began to dawn whitely, they finally fell asleep.

Eventually, the night dawned.
The man woke his eyes. The morning sun was sparkling into the room.
"Ah, I slept well."
While stretching, looking at the wife lying beside him, with a "Ah!" of shock, he, while holding his clothes, jumped down. It was not the cute former wife, but was the remains of a white skeleton piled up.
He in a hurry put on his hakama and ran out, ran into the neighbor's house, and with a face knowing nothing, asked:
"The person in this next door, where is she now?"
"Oh, the next door person? Whatever it was, one woman lived there, but the husband who had been visiting for four or five years heartlessly abandoned her and went somewhere, so she grieved deeply, and finally became sick, but since there was not even one person to nurse her, she died this summer. Since there is no person to perform a funeral, the corpse likely remains as it is."
The person who knew nothing spoke thus to him.

**The Nun's Lustful Obsession**

"Oh?" he thought suspiciously, but without knowing it, all through the night he listened intently to that voice of sutra-reading.
Eventually, the night began to dawn.

"What could it be?"
and, while still looking well, the night completely dawned. It was a single rock covered in moss and with vines fully wound around it.
"Really, who is reciting the sutra? Perhaps in this rock an immortal is and is reading the sutra."
Thinking this, the rock itself came to look solemn for some reason, so he stopped his feet and watched that rock intently.
And, after staring at the rock for a while, suddenly of its own accord it began to move "gura-gura" (shaking), became gradually tall, and while watching, became a human shape and began to run.
