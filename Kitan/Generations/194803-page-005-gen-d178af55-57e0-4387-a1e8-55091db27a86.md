## Cultural & Historical Annotations

1.  **Special Service Agency (特務機関 - Tokumu Kikan):** These were the infamous intelligence and paramilitary units of the Imperial Japanese Army. In post-war pulp fiction, they are almost always portrayed as villainous, shadowy organizations involved in torture, assassination, and vice.
2.  **Shanghai Context:** During the late 1930s, Shanghai was a "city of spies," divided into international settlements. The mention of "Jai Alai" (ハイアライ) refers to the famous Jai Alai auditorium in the French Concession, a notorious gambling den and hive of underworld activity.
3.  **Manila Massacres:** The text explicitly mentions war crimes in Manila. This reflects the mid-50s Japanese consciousness grappling with the atrocities committed by the IJA, often refracted through the lens of sensationalist literature.
4.  **Erotic Walking Sticks:** A common "ero-guro" trope. These were canes with hidden lenses or compartments containing "spring pictures" (shunga) or erotic photographs, symbolizing the hidden, lewd underside of "respectable" society.
