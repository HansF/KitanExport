However, one day when that Kenji went to his workplace, he encountered a model of such beauty that it shook even his stoic heart.
Her nose was high, her eyes narrow, her skin white; she was beautiful like a ukiyo-e print. However, Kenji-kun exchanged a word or two and was shocked. Her greeting was like "strapping a nose with wood" [extremely curt], the complete opposite of her facial features and appearance; he was brushed off sharply with a harsh manner. It seems beautiful flowers must have thorns, but right from the first meeting, it was an excessively insolent attitude.
—"Damn it! I'll take her down a notch once!"
Kenji-kun decided so in his heart. While hardening the plaster and reproducing her beautiful facial features, he wondered, "Now then, just how can I humble this arrogant woman?" He fell into thought.
—"How exhilarating it would be if I could make this woman perform *dogeza* [prostrate kneeling] on the ground."
Only such thoughts spun round and round in his head.
Two or three days passed after that, and he suddenly struck his knee [had an epiphany]. An inspiration had suddenly been obtained. In a magazine a neighborhood child was reading, the characters "Iron Mask" were written, standing out largely.

—"A mask! Interesting!"
Kenji-kun wore a smirk.
The plaster statues of her—that is, Miss Oi Yukari—the head and body, are at the company. How about making a doll exactly like her, decorating the floor with it, making a mask that fits it perfectly out of something suitable, putting it on the doll to show her, or if possible, putting it on her herself to make her suffer?
—"That's it! This is a good idea."
He became absorbed in thought. And after hesitating over this and that, he finally put together a plan. For the mask material, he decided for the time being to use rigid vinyl chloride. The reason being that, due to the nature of this vinyl, a mask can be easily made just by heating it and draping it over the plaster statue. Furthermore, if he used transparent vinyl, the face would be visible when worn, and if he adjusted the thickness, it would adhere closely to the skin. Therefore, air would not enter from the contact surface. He realized that because of this, voices would likely not leak from the mouth.
Kenji-kun leaned over his desk, resting his chin on his hands, casting vacant eyes out the window as he pursued this idea concretely.
—"If I cover from the lower jaw to the forehead with this mask, naturally, the movement of the jaw will be stopped, and it will become a perfect gag. But if she can't breathe, that would be bad, so I must open nose holes. It seems more interesting to keep the eye areas open too, and it might be good to open the lip area a little and stick or remove vinyl tape there."
As his thoughts progressed, his eyes sparkled with increasing liveliness. And he stood up with vigor.
Three days later—Kenji-kun was testing the fit of the newly completed mask by putting it on the bust of Yukari decorated on his desk. The mask has strings attached at six locations. He smirked.
It was finished. The cursed mask—. All that remains is to request the "appearance" of that arrogant beauty. Holding the transparent mask in his hand, he was somewhat tense.

Saturday afternoon, Kenji-kun stopped Yukari in the company hallway.
"Do you want something?"
A curt soprano, unsuited to her beautiful lips, echoed.
—"Damn! Just you wait!"
Kenji-kun, having a secret scheme in his belly, spoke casually.
"Actually, I was allowed to make a mannequin of you by myself for the first time. So, as a memento, I made a bust of you and have it displayed at my house. If you like, I'm thinking I'd like to give it as a gift, so would you mind taking a look for a moment?"
"Oh! Is that so... Since it's your work anyway..."

The thought obsessed him, spinning in his mind like a fever dream. Two days later, he struck his knee in a flash of inspiration. He had seen it in a magazine a neighborhood boy was reading—the words "The Iron Mask" had leapt off the page.

*A mask! Of course!*

A dark smirk crept across Kenji’s face. He already had the plaster molds for Miss Yukari Oi’s head and torso at the office. What if he crafted a perfect likeness, a doll to keep in his room? And then, what if he fashioned a mask specifically for it—or better yet, a mask to force onto the woman herself, to watch her squirm in silence?

*Yes! That’s the ticket!*

He lost himself in the technicalities. After much deliberation, he settled on a plan. He would use rigid vinyl chloride. ## Cultural & Historical Annotations

*   **Iron Mask (鉄仮面):** A reference to the French legend/Dumas novel, which was popular in Japanese magazines as a symbol of confinement and hidden identity.
*   **Western vs. Japanese Beauty:** The text notes the shift in Showa-era aesthetics toward Western looks, positioning the "Japanese-style beauty" (Yamato Nadeshiko look) as something rare and highly prized, which heightens Kenji's desire to "conquer" her.
*   **Dogeza (土下座):** The ultimate act of submission in Japanese culture. Kenji’s desire to see a beautiful woman do this highlights the power-dynamic/humiliation aspect of the fantasy.
*   **Vinyl (塩化ビニール):** In the 1950s, plastics and vinyl were "modern" materials. Their use in fetish gear (masks, raincoats) was a burgeoning subgenre within the *ero-guro* movement.

## Lexical Notes & Euphemisms

*   **木で鼻をくくる (Ki de hana o kukuru):** Literally "to strap the nose with wood." An idiom meaning to be extremely curt, blunt, or cold.
*   **剣もほろろ (Ken mo hororo):** To give a cold shoulder; to be brusque.
*   **高慢ちき (Koman-chiki):** A colloquial, somewhat derogatory term for "stuck-up" or "arrogant."
*   **猿ぐつわ (Sarugutsuwa):** A gag. Literally "monkey bit."
*   **腹に一物 (Hara ni ichimotsu):** To have a secret motive or a dark scheme "in one's belly."
